Periodization training is used to create "peak" week for an athlete. During this week the athlete has a greater likelihood of having elevated performance. The "peak" week follows directly after the final rest week of a training plan.

Getting the most out of your plan - when and how to vary intensity

Base week: Average volume and intensity

Loading Phase: Increased volume and intensity

Recovery Phase: Reduced volume and intensity

Peak Phase - Competition: Close to average volume and intensity

How to increase intensity

  • Time constraints on how long you have to shoot each arrow or end
  • Scoring
  • Practice head-to-head rounds
  • Simulate competition in your head, example: think "I need to shoot a 9 to stay in this"
How to lower intensity

  • Shooting blank bale
  • Focusing on the feeling of the shot over scores
  • Focusing on flow instead of control
